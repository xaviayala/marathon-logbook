# 🦴 The Pain-Free Protocol: 0–10 Scale

> **The Brutal Truth:** Most athletes lie to themselves about the 0–10 scale. They call a "4" a "2" to keep running, which is how a simple "twinge" becomes a "stress fracture." 

For this **Sub-3:15 Marathon Project**, the Numerical Rating Scale (NRS) is our ultimate guardrail. It isn't just about how much it hurts—it's about how much the pain **interferes with your running mechanics.**



### 📊 The Athlete's Reality Check

| Level | Category | What it feels like | The "Coach" Verdict |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0** | **None** | Complete silence from the body. You feel only the road. | **The Target.** 100% of energy goes to forward motion. |
| **1** | **Very Mild** | A "whisper." You feel a spot, but it disappears after warm-up. | **Safe.** Monitor it. Likely minor inflammation. |
| **2** | **Mild** | Noticeable but doesn't change your stride. Easy to ignore. | **The Limit.** Maximum allowed for a "Pain-Free" project. |
| **3** | **Tolerable** | Annoying. You think about it every few minutes. | **Warning.** Body needs rest or extra Isometrics. |
| **4** | **Distressing** | It’s always there. You are "running around" the pain (compensating). | **STOP.** You are damaging form. Secondary injury imminent. |
| **5** | **Moderate** | Pain is the dominant thought. Hard to focus on pace or HR. | **Critical Failure.** Continuing is purely an ego move. |
| **6** | **Intense** | You are physically grimacing. Your pace is dropping. | **Injury Territory.** You have crossed into tissue damage. |
| **7** | **Severe** | You are limping. You cannot maintain a running gait. | **Shut it down.** You are risking months of rehab. |
| **8** | **Very Severe** | Nausea from pain. Can't walk 1 km without stopping. | **Medical Issue.** Likely a tear or a stress fracture. |
| **9** | **Excruciating** | Cannot stand. Thinking is difficult. | **ER Visit.** |
| **10** | **Unimaginable** | Passing out. Total systemic shock. | **N/A.** You won't be checking your Garmin. |

### 🧠 Operational Guidelines (45+ Athlete)

1.  **The Green Zone (0–2):** Proceed with training. Your -10kg weight loss and Soleus/Core/Glute work are providing the necessary durability for your Sub-3:15 engine.
2.  **The Yellow Zone (3):** **No running tomorrow.** Double down on **Soleus Isometrics** and **Glute** work. If it stays at 3 for two consecutive sessions, the "Coach" script will trigger a mandatory rest week.
3.  **The Red Zone (4+):** The audit is ruthless. If your training log shows a 4 and you ran anyway, you are sabotaging your marathon start line and your long-term health. **Immediate cessation of running required.**
